## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are internship and alternance recruiters, hiring managers, and technical interviewers evaluating a French Computer Science student for AI, ML, data science, and software engineering roles. They will usually skim quickly, so the interface should help them understand strengths, project quality, and readiness in seconds. The core job to be done is to make Katib stand out, signal professionalism and technical depth, and make it easy to verify credibility through projects, resume, contact details, and highlighted DataCamp certificates.

### Brand Personality
Professional, ambitious, polished. The voice should feel credible, modern, and career-focused rather than flashy or playful. The interface should evoke confidence first, then curiosity and momentum, with a strong sense of seriousness and craft suitable for recruiters assessing internship potential.

### Aesthetic Direction
Aim for a modern product-design feel inspired by Vercel and Linear: dark-first, high visual hierarchy, refined spacing, clean typography, restrained but intentional motion, and premium-looking surfaces. Light mode should still be polished and usable, but the visual system should feel strongest in dark mode. Avoid generic template-portfolio patterns, overused hero treatments, and anything that reads like a bland startup landing page, playful Dribbble concept, or flat academic CV page. DataCamp certificates should be treated as credibility assets and surfaced clearly without overpowering project work.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with credibility: prioritize evidence of skill through projects, metrics, certificates, resume access, and clear internship availability.
2. Create fast recruiter scanability: use strong hierarchy, concise copy, and obvious sectioning so value is clear within a few seconds.
3. Feel premium, not generic: favor deliberate composition, refined dark surfaces, and distinctive restraint over template-like portfolio tropes.
4. Use motion with discipline: subtle animations should support hierarchy and polish, never distract from content.
5. Preserve accessibility: maintain at least WCAG AA contrast and keep interactions legible and comfortable in both dark-first and light-mode presentations.
